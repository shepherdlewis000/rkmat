WE FIX CHICAGO CHIMNEYS

Chicago winters and our freeze-thaw cycle are detrimental to brick and stone mortar work. Chimneys are exposed to water which causes deterioration and cause cracks and breakdown of brick and mortar. 

They don’t call Chicago the “Windy City” for nothing, and wind can damage your chimney along with water. Most chimneys require at least some repair after 20-30 years of exposure & chimneys require periodic inspection to ensure safety and integrity.

The National Fire Protection Association Standard 211 says, "Chimneys, fireplaces, and vents shall be inspected at least once a year for soundness, freedom from deposits, and correct clearances. Cleaning, maintenance, and repairs shall be done if necessary."

Allowing your chimney to go unchecked can lend to unsafe indoor air quality and the threat of further property damage such as chimney fires and water seepage. Lack of inspection can cost you in the long run! We evaluate and inspect Chicago chimneys.

Chimney cap & chimney flashing repair

Chimney caps (also called chimney crowns or rain covers) protect from the elements and keep birds and bats out. An attractive chimney cap can add curb value to your home. A proper chimney cap can stop dangerous gases and drafts from entering your home.

Chimney flashing is the seal between the roofing material and the chimney. It prevents rain water from running down the chimney where it can damage rafters, walls, joists and other elements of your structure. Leaking chimneys are often caused by faulty flashing.

We help you choose the right chimney cap and offer chimney cap installation, replacement and repair. If after an inspection it's found that your chimney flashing is failing, we'll offer a quick repair or replacement of this flashing.

Waterproofing Chicago's leaking chimneys

Dampness in your attic is often the first sign of chimney trouble, usually noticed after it rains or some time in the rainy seasons. Hairline cracks can occur even in new construction because of mortar shrinking during curing. Masonry materials are porous and absorb water. Defective mortar joints and deteriorating brick result and lead to even greater water damage. We can discuss options with you to stop water damage and save you from major rebuilding your entire chimney in the future!
